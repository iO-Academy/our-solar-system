Our Solar System

Our solar system consists of a single star and various astronomical objects that are bound to it by gravity. The objects include planets, dwarf planets, asteroids, comets, and meteoroids.

The word "solar" comes from the Latin "solaris", which means "sun".

Asteroids

Asteroids are rocky objects left over from the formation of the solar system over 4.5 billion years ago.

There are over 1.2 million asteroids.

Asteroid on Wikipedia

Comets

Comets are icy objects of frozen gases, rocks and dust left over from the formation of the solar system.

When a comet nears the Sun, it warms up and releases gas and dust that creates the characteristic tail.

Comet on Wikipedia

Meteoroids

A meteoroid is a small rock or metal object in space. They range in size from dust grains to a meter wide.

When a meteoroid enters the Earth's atmosphere it burns up. We call this a meteor or shooting star.
